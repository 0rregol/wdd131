"It's not about winning, it's about fighting to the end"

Derek Anthony Redmond

Quick Facts

  • Born: September 3, 1965
  • Nationality: British
  • Sport: Athletics (400m)
  • Olympics: 1988, 1992

The Barcelona Moment

During the 400m semifinal at the 1992 Barcelona Olympics, Redmond tore his hamstring halfway through the race and collapsed in pain. Determined to finish, he got up and began hobbling toward the finish line.

His father, Jim Redmond, rushed from the stands to help his son. Together, they completed the race to a standing ovation from the 65,000-strong crowd, creating one of the most iconic moments in Olympic history.

"I'm the proudest father alive. I'm prouder of him than I would have been if he had won the gold medal."

- Jim Redmond

Career Highlights

1986 European Championships

Gold medal in 4×400m relay

1991 World Championships

Silver medal in 4×400m relay

British Records

Former British record holder in 400m

Legacy

Derek Redmond's story transcends sports. His determination in Barcelona became a universal symbol of perseverance. After retiring from athletics, he became a motivational speaker, sharing his inspiring story with audiences worldwide.

His moment with his father remains one of the most replayed Olympic clips, embodying the true spirit of the Games - not just winning, but the human struggle and dignity in competition.
