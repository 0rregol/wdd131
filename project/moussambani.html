"I didn't come to win, I came to represent my country with honor"

Éric Moussambani

Key Facts

  • Born: 1978
  • Nationality: Equatoguinean
  • Sport: Swimming (100m freestyle)
  • Olympics: Sydney 2000

The Most Famous 100m

Moussambani, who had barely trained in a small pool, qualified through an Olympic invitation. In his heat, the other two swimmers were disqualified for false starts, leaving him alone.

He completed the 100m freestyle in 1:52.72 (nearly double the finalists' time), but the crowd gave him a standing ovation for his effort, nicknaming him "Eric the Eel".

"The last 15 meters were very difficult... but I knew my whole country was watching me."

- Éric Moussambani

Later Achievements

Personal Best

Improved his time to 57 seconds

Coach

Became national team coach

Inspiration

Icon of Olympic spirit

Legacy

Moussambani showed that the Olympics aren't just for the best athletes, but for those who represent the values of perseverance. His story inspired athletes from small nations.

Today he's technical director of Equatorial Guinea's Swimming Federation.
