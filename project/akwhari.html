"My country did not send me to start the race, they sent me to finish it"

John Stephen Akhwari

Key Facts

  • Born: 1938
  • Nationality: Tanzanian
  • Sport: Athletics (Marathon)
  • Olympics: Mexico 1968

The 1968 Mexico City Marathon

During the Olympic marathon at high-altitude Mexico City, Akhwari suffered a bad fall that left him with an injured knee and dislocated shoulder. Despite the pain, he kept running.

He crossed the finish line long after everyone else, with the stadium nearly empty, but his determination made him a symbol of the true Olympic spirit.

"I don't think there's anything special about what I did. All athletes came to Mexico to compete. I had to finish."

- John Akhwari

Legacy

Akhwari proved that courage isn't measured in medals, but in the ability to overcome pain and adversity. His story remains one of the most touching in Olympic history.

Today, he's a symbol of perseverance in Tanzania and the world of athletics.
